            <div class="sepration"><h3>History</h3></div>
            <p class="p">

                St. Thomas School was established by Mr. Rakesh Chattree the Principal of Christ Church College, Lucknow, who laid its foundation on the 8th day of July in the year 2008 and got it dedicated on 5th December, 2009. The School was first named as Bishop Stephen School in 2009 which was later changed to St. Thomas School in the year 2010 on the name of the Church standing in the middle of the campus since 1859.
                <br><br>
                St. Thomas School got registered under the Christ Church Mc Conoghey School Society, under the Societies Registration Act 21 of 1860 and is governed by the Diocese of Lucknow (CNI) having its head office at 25-M.G. Road, Allahbad.
                <br><br>
                The School got recognized by the Education Department of Uttar Pradesh on 30th December, 2017, its registration number being 11031-35/2017-18.</p>

           <p>St David is the patron saint of Wales, whose saint's day is celebrated annually on 1 March. His birth date is uncertain: suggestions range from 462 to 512. He is traditionally believed to be the son of Saint Non and the grandson of Ceredig ap Cunedda, king of Ceredigion. The Welsh annals placed his death 569 years after the birth of Christ, but Phillimore’s dating revised this to 601.</p>
               
           <p>St David was born on the south west coast of Wales, near the present day city of St Davids. His family was aristocratic. He was educated at a monastery and became a missionary, travelling through Wales and to south west England and Brittany to spread Christianity. He is also supposed to have visited Rome and Jerusalem. David founded a monastery where St Davids stands today. He died in 589 AD and was buried in the grounds of his own monastery. He has been the patron saint of Wales since the 12th century.
        </p>

A white dove, which became his emblem, was seen settling on his shoulder.</p>

He became one of the most venerated saints and megalo - martyrs in Christianity, and was especially venerated by the Crusaders.</p>

           <p>Saint Patrick, the patron saint of Ireland, was a fifth-century Romano-British Christian missionary and Bishop in Ireland, also known as “Apostle of Ireland”.</p>
               
           <p>His father, Calpornius, was a Roman-British army officer and a deacon. Despite his family involvement in the church, the young Patrick was not a believer. His life was ordinary, and completely unexceptional, until the age of 16 when he was kidnapped, by Irish pirates and sold into slavery in Ireland. The next six years were spent imprisoned in the north of the island and he worked as a herdsmen of sheep and pigs on Mount Slemish in Co. Antrim.</p>
        
        <p>During this period, he became increasingly religious. After a vision led him to stow away on a boat bound for Britain, Patrick escaped back to his family. But then he had a dream that the Irish were calling him back to Ireland to tell them about God. This inspired him to return to Ireland as a priest, where he was active as a missionary during the second half of the 5th century. Early medieval tradition credits him with being the founder of Christianity in Ireland, converting a society practicing a form of Celtic polytheism. </p>
        <p>For twenty years he travelled the length and breadth of the island, baptizing people and establishing monasteries, schools and churches as he went. By the time he died on 17th March 461 (or 493), he left behind an organized church, the see of Armagh, and an island of Christians. This date – 17 March – has been commemorated as St Patrick's Day ever since.</p>
